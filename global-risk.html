FAQ

What is the Global Risk Index?

The Global Risk Index is a simplified daily indicator that tracks broad global pressure across geopolitical conflict, economic stress, public health signals and climate-related risks.

How is the score calculated?

Fresh confirmed events add pressure. Older headlines are discounted unless they continue to create measurable risk. The score is directional, not an official forecast.

What does an elevated score mean?

An elevated score means several global risk channels are active at the same time. It does not predict one specific event.
